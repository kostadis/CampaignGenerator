# Your Character's Voice

After each session, a script generates the session document — a written account of what happened, told in first-person by each of your characters in turn. Think of it like a novel where Chapter 1 is Aldric, Chapter 2 is Syreth, and so on.

By default the script does its best to match each character's voice based on what they said and did during the session. But you can give it better material to work with by writing a short **voice file** for your character.

---

## What goes in a voice file

A voice file is just a plain text note — a paragraph or two — describing how your character thinks and speaks from the inside. You're not writing the story; you're giving the narrator instructions.

Think about:

- **Sentence rhythm** — does your character think in long winding thoughts, or short punchy ones? Do they finish their sentences or trail off?
- **What they notice** — does your character notice people's faces, the smell of a place, the sound of footsteps, the weight of a weapon?
- **What they never say** — words or frames that feel wrong for them. ("She never says 'feel' — she says 'know'.")
- **A recurring internal habit** — something they always do when they're scared, angry, or uncertain
- **Their relationship to their own past** — do they run from it, lean into it, intellectualize it?
- **Humor, if any** — dry? Self-deprecating? Oblivious?

You don't need to cover all of these. Two or three specific things beat a long vague description.

---

## Examples

### A simple, direct warrior

> Aldric thinks in short sentences. He doesn't explain himself — he decides, then acts. When memories come up he doesn't dwell on them; they land like a fact and he moves on. He uses physical language: weight, iron, stone, cold. He's not humorless but he'd never make a joke on purpose.

---

### A sardonic observer

> Syreth narrates from a slight distance, as if she's watching herself as much as the scene. She notices social dynamics before she notices anything else — who defers to whom, who's pretending. Her inner voice is dry and a little cruel, including toward herself. She grew up in a world where showing weakness was an invitation, and that never fully left her. She doesn't italicize her feelings; she buries them in observations about other people.

---

### An earnest healer

> Mira's inner voice is warm but never sentimental. She notices when people are in pain before they say anything. She asks herself questions she doesn't answer — "What did he mean by that?" — and then moves on. She finds humor in small absurd details. She doesn't like conflict but she doesn't avoid it; she just tries to understand it first. She thinks of her magic as something she borrows, not something she owns.

---

### A loud, literal barbarian

> Gorvan thinks in big simple truths. He doesn't do irony. When something is funny he says it's funny; when something is wrong he says it's wrong. He gets distracted by food and drink at inopportune moments and is not embarrassed about this. His emotional range is wide but his vocabulary for emotions is small — he'll describe complex grief as "a stone in his chest" because that's the closest word he has. He respects people who are direct with him and distrusts people who aren't.

---

## How to write yours

Write it however comes naturally — prose, bullet points, a mix. The main thing is to be **specific**. "He's mysterious and quiet" is less useful than "He answers most questions with another question, and he's aware that people find this annoying."

It doesn't have to be long. Three specific sentences will do more than a page of generalities.

If there's a line of dialogue your character said — at the table or that you've imagined them saying — that perfectly captures their voice, include it. Real examples are gold.

---

## Submitting your file

Send your voice file (as a `.txt` or `.md` file, or just pasted in a message) to the DM before the next session document is generated. Name it after your character — `aldric.md`, `syreth.md`, etc.

You can update it any time. If your character has changed — grown, broken, shifted — update the voice file to match.

---

*Your voice file is only ever used to shape your character's narration. It is never shown to other players.*
